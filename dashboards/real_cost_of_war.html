The Real Cost of a War

Textbooks show troop counts and battle maps. They rarely show what wars cost in dollars, debt, and lives per objective achieved. These six conflicts tell a story about how the economics of war changed when America stopped drafting and started borrowing.

~$8T

The structural shift nobody talks about: WWII was financed through war bonds, income taxes as high as 94%, and rationing — Americans felt it. Post-9/11 wars were financed entirely through deficit spending, fought by a volunteer military representing <1% of the population, with tax cuts passed simultaneously. The result: $4.2 trillion spent, 6,892 US deaths, and outcomes that inverted within months of withdrawal. When war has no visible cost to most citizens, the threshold to start one gets lower.
